Chapter 4-Operators

Operators in C are symbols that represent computations, comparisons, or actions on one or more operands. Here's an overview of the various types of operators in C:

Arithmetic Operators:

addition (+), subtraction (-), multiplication(*), division (\)

realtional Operators:

equal to (==), not equal to (!=), greater than(>),less than (<), greater than equal to (>=), less than or equal to (<=)

logical Operators:

logical and (&&), logical or (||),logical not(!):Combine multiple conditions and perform logical operations:

assignment Operators:

=, +=, -=, *=, /=, %=: Assign values to variables and perform arithmetic operations simultaneously.

increment and decrement operator:

++ (Increment), -- (Decrement): Increase or decrease the value of a variable by 1.

bitwise Operators:

& (Bitwise AND), | (Bitwise OR), ^ (Bitwise XOR), ~ (Bitwise NOT), << (Left shift), >> (Right shift): Perform operations at the bit level.

Understanding and using operators effectively is crucial for performing computations, comparisons, and logical operations in C programming. They play a key role in manipulating data and controlling program flow.
